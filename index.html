æbleskiver History

Denmark, the most southerly country in the Nordic Region, is a small, densely populated country made up of a large number of islands skirted by lovely beaches. Denmark borders Sweden to the east and Germany to the south.

One of the most popular Danish Christmas treats is æbleskiver—essentially pancake balls. The name literally means “apple slices” in Danish, as they were originally filled with small pieces of apple or applesauce. Over time the filling disappeared and we got the æbleskive we know today.

Family Background

My great-grandparents on my dad’s side, with the last name of Kamp, migrated to the United States during the Gold Rush. The Kamps settled in the same small town that I grew up in, called Danville, California. One of the traditions that has been passed down through our family is making æbleskivers, a Danish treat. The recipe still comes out for every special occasion, especially on Christmas morning.
